Managing a recall for a previous offender

What we did

We focused on 2 key steps: initiate a recall and assess a recall.

We looked at the cases where that recall is initiated, processed by the PPCS, involved parties are informed of the revocation order, and then the offender is either unlawfully at large, or is brought back into custody.

Initiate a recall - Community Offender Managers

Currently Community offender managers have to fill in an 8 pages form (PART A) to initiate a recall.
We designed an online form that automatically pulls some data about the offender, and the Community offender manager can send it to their senior manager to sign the recall off.

Assess a recall - PPCS team

PPCS caseworkers will receive this request on their to-do list, with all the information and data available to assess the recall and communicate the decision to all the parties involved.

What we didn't do

  • Parole Board reviews. We left them out of scope for this Sprint because we have enough learnings on that process and needs from the investigations done in the Release sprints.
  • Rethinking the process to initiate a recall, as this will need a discovery. We’ve focussed our efforts on improving and simplifying this step as much as we could so the migration is feasible.

What we learned

  • Pulling data where possible keeps proving useful and time saving but expectations are based on past experiences. In order to feel confident when making a decision, participants still wanted control and being able to see as much information as possible, not just what was surfaced for them.
  • Fewer steps and reuse of data will bring more consistency to recall requests. For it to work the interface needs to signal to users where the information comes from and explain what they don’t need to do anymore.
  • Decisions that rely on people having a conversation are hard to standardise. The interface should collect clear explanations with the right metadata.

To explore further

Regardless of their role or band, people largely need access to the same information about a recall (although for different purposes) This currently makes the recall process have more unique stages than a future state may require. When PPUD is replaced there may be the need for only minimal (or potentially no) rekeying or manual cross-referencing of information. This creates a good opportunity to explore teams/roles and where people could spend their time on more useful activities
